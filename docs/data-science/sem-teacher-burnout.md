# SEM WarpPLS — Teacher Burnout & Self-Concept Analysis

> Investigating the structural relationship between burnout symptoms and self-concept among teachers using Structural Equation Modeling.

## Overview

This research project applies **Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)** with the WarpPLS method to analyze the relationship between burnout symptoms and self-concept among teachers. The study aims to uncover how teacher self-perception relates to professional burnout indicators.

## Problem Statement

Teacher burnout is a growing concern in education systems worldwide. Understanding the **structural relationship between self-concept and burnout** can help design effective intervention programs. This study uses advanced multivariate analysis to model these latent constructs and their interconnections.

## Tech Stack & Tools

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| WarpPLS | SEM analysis with non-linear relationships |
| SPSS | Descriptive statistics and data validation |
| R / Python | Supporting analysis |
| Survey methodology | Primary data collection |

## Methodology & Approach

### 1. Research Design
- Cross-sectional survey design
- Structured questionnaire with validated scales
- Likert-scale measurement instruments

### 2. Data Collection
- Teacher sample selection with appropriate sampling method
- Administered burnout and self-concept measurement instruments
- Data cleaning and validity/reliability testing

### 3. SEM Analysis with WarpPLS
- **Measurement model** evaluation (convergent & discriminant validity)
- **Structural model** path analysis
- Non-linear relationship detection (WarpPLS advantage)
- Model fit assessment (APC, ARS, AVIF)

## Key Results & Insights

- Identified significant structural paths between burnout dimensions and self-concept
- WarpPLS revealed **non-linear relationships** that traditional linear SEM would miss
- Findings contribute to understanding teacher well-being from a psychological perspective
- Results inform intervention design for educational institutions

## Future Improvements

- Longitudinal study design to establish causal relationships
- Expand sample size and geographic diversity
- Include mediating variables (e.g., social support, workload)
- Compare with CB-SEM (AMOS/LISREL) results
