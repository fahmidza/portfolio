# My Journey into Data Science

When I first enrolled in the Statistics program at Universitas Gadjah Mada, I saw numbers as abstract concepts in textbooks. Four years later, I see them as stories waiting to be told — patterns hidden in noise, signals that can drive real-world decisions.

<!-- truncate -->

## Where It All Started

My journey began with traditional statistics — hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and probability theory. These fundamentals built a strong foundation, but it was when I discovered **Python and machine learning** that everything clicked. Suddenly, the statistical concepts I learned in class had powerful computational tools to bring them to life.

## The Turning Point

Two experiences transformed my perspective:

### 1. Leading Komunitas Satria Data UGM
As president of UGM's data community, I led a 27-member team and helped our university achieve **4th place nationally in Satria Data 2024**. Organizing study groups, workshops, and competition prep sessions taught me that data science is as much about **collaboration and communication** as it is about algorithms.

### 2. My Internship at PT Bina Pertiwi (Astra)
Working as a Data Analyst Intern at an Astra subsidiary was my first real taste of industry data work. Building real-time dashboards in Google Data Studio that actual stakeholders used for decision-making — that's when I understood the gap between academic projects and production-ready analytics.

## What I've Learned

After 12+ projects, 6 competition awards, and 2 internships, here are my key takeaways:

1. **Domain knowledge matters more than fancy algorithms.** Understanding the business context transforms a good model into an impactful solution.

2. **Communication is a superpower.** The best analysis is worthless if you can't explain it to non-technical stakeholders. Data storytelling is an essential skill.

3. **Start simple, iterate fast.** My best-performing models often started as simple baselines. Complexity should be justified by results, not ego.

4. **The data pipeline is 80% of the work.** Data cleaning, preprocessing, and feature engineering consume far more time than model training — and they have a bigger impact on results.

5. **Open source and community are everything.** Every project I've built stands on the shoulders of open-source tools and community knowledge.

## What's Next

I'm currently focused on deepening my expertise in:
- **Deep learning** for computer vision and NLP applications
- **MLOps** for deploying and monitoring models in production
- **Statistical consulting** for research and business applications
